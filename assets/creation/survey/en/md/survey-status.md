# Cross-Domain Structural Similarity Survey: Overview

## 1. Purpose of the Survey

There is a hypothesis: that creation — from quantum fluctuations to the emergence of life, the workings of the mind, and social transformations — is a process sharing the same underlying structure. That this structure can be described as five stages: Field → Wave → Relation → Vortex → Bundle.

To examine this hypothesis, we conducted a survey spanning 30 academic disciplines. We analyzed whether the theories and concepts accumulated in each field point to the same structure as these five stages. This is not a "proof." It is an effort to determine whether the same structure can be seen in what different disciplines have described using different vocabularies.

## 2. Methodology

For each of the 30 academic disciplines, we selected approximately 10 theories or concepts and analyzed their structural correspondence with the five stages. The selection criterion was "a major theory in that field relating to creation, emergence, or change" — we did not cherry-pick theories that resemble the five stages.

The analysis proceeded as follows. First, we examined which parts of the five stages each theory's described structure corresponds to. Next, we documented not only the correspondences but also the gaps — elements present in the five stages but absent from prior research, and vice versa. Furthermore, an independent AI system conducted a review to detect analytical bias.

**On methodological limitations.** Much of this survey involves AI-mediated interpretation. The structural correspondences with prior research are interpretive hypotheses, not definitive mappings based on close reading of original texts. The labels for the five stages (Field, Wave, Relation, Vortex, Bundle) are convenient divisions along a gradient, as are the labels from prior research. Therefore, correspondences always involve gradation and should be understood as approximations rather than strict one-to-one mappings. Some portions have not yet been verified through close reading by the author.

## 3. Overview of the Five-Stage Model

Below is a concise summary of the five stages referenced in this survey.

**Field** — A state where nothing has yet occurred. No sense of dissonance. Zero, void. Only potential exists.

**Wave** — Fluctuation arises. Opposition and difference emerge, and a sense that something is off begins to take shape.

**Relation** — Contradiction is maximized. Relationships that cannot settle into either side are woven together. Whether one can remain on the boundary determines the quality of creation.

**Vortex** — Coherence emerges. A new structure appears while still containing contradiction.

**Bundle** — Direction is established, and a reusable structure remains. However, it is not fixed — it circulates as input into the next "Field."

This is not a linear process. The return from Bundle to Field — accepting new fluctuations with the born structure as a premise — is what sustains creation.

## 4. Cross-Domain Findings

Through the survey of 30 domains, several findings recurred across disciplinary boundaries.

**"Relation" has the greatest discriminating power.** Concepts corresponding to Field, Wave, Vortex, and Bundle can be found in many domains, but theories that explicitly describe "Relation" — remaining at the boundary where contradiction is maximized — are limited. Whether a correspondence to Relation exists most sharply discriminates the precision of structural similarity with the five stages.

**Wallas's model in creativity research** (Preparation → Incubation → Illumination → Verification): Incubation corresponds to the Field-to-Wave domain, Illumination to the Relation-to-Vortex domain, and Verification to Bundle. The basic structure of alternation between conscious work and unconscious processing is shared. The five-stage model has an explicit "Relation" stage. In Wallas's description, the transition between Incubation and Illumination — the state where contradiction is maximized and cannot converge in either direction — is not structurally described.

**Self-organized criticality in complexity science** (Per Bak) embeds the cyclicality of the five stages within the theory itself. The cycle of accumulation → criticality → avalanche → re-accumulation physically describes the return from Bundle to Field. The concept of "universality classes" (Wilson) also provides a physics-based suggestion for the fundamental question of this survey: why the same macroscopic structure appears in different microscopic systems.

**"The flower that is hidden" in performing arts** (Zeami) formulates that retention — holding something as a question rather than processing it reflexively — is not mere suppression but a source of creative value, as one of the world's oldest aesthetic treatises. The "Yes, and..." principle in improvisational theater functions as an operational definition of Relation. This principle of accepting a partner's offer while adding a new element embodies in a single phrase the essence of Relation: maintaining indeterminacy within relationships while generating development.

**"Boundaries are zones" in architecture** — this finding was independently confirmed from fiber scale to urban scale. Alexander (thick outlines), van Eyck (in-between), the Japanese engawa, and Lynch (urban edge) all describe boundaries not as lines but as regions with width. Through mutual complementation with liminality in anthropology (transitional periods are zones), this finding holds the strongest cross-domain support among the 30 domains.

## 5. Survey Depth

We are proceeding with the policy of surveying all 30 domains to the same depth. However, progress varies across domains. Eleven domains (Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Evolutionary Biology, Earth Science, Astronomy, Engineering, Neuroscience, Life Sciences, Clinical Medicine, Pharmacy) are finalized versions with independent review completed. The remaining domains have completed analysis and are in the process of cross-referencing with independent reviews.

Detailed survey results for each domain are published individually as domain reports.

## 6. Individual Survey Reports

For each of the 30 domains, we have prepared domain reports summarizing the analysis of selected theories, correspondence with the five stages, and unique findings. Readers with interest in specific domains are encouraged to consult those reports.

For an index and overview of all domains, see [Cross-Domain Structural Similarity Survey: Domain Index](survey-domain-index.md).

## 7. Summary

From the survey of 30 domains, concepts structurally corresponding to the five-stage model were widely confirmed. In particular, the fact that "Relation" — remaining at the boundary where contradiction is maximized — is independently described in many domains suggests the possibility that this structure is not confined to any specific field.

However, all of these correspondences remain at the stage of interpretive hypothesis. They are observations that "appear to point to the same structure," not confirmations that "they are the same structure." It is the stance of this survey to report honestly, including the differences between domains where correspondence was confirmed and those where it remains ambiguous.

Specific analyses for each domain can be found in the domain reports. We encourage readers to begin with the domain that interests them most.
