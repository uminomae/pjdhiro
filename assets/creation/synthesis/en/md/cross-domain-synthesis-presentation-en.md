## Theme 1: Typology of En

**Supported by 28/30 fields** — The most powerful cross-cutting finding

| Type | Representative |

## Theme 2: Threshold Structure

**Directly supported by 11 fields** — Conditions for the En-to-Uzu transition

The nature of thresholds varies by field:

- **Quantitative and irreversible**: Physics (phase transitions), Astronomy (Chandrasekhar limit)
- **Controllable**: Chemistry (barrier reduction via catalysis)
- **Probabilistic**: Complex systems (SOC criticality)
- **Developmental**: Religious studies, Developmental psychology

**Key finding**: **10 fields describe "Uzu-unreached pathways."** Not all En lead to Uzu. Failure and incompletion are structurally built in.

## Theme 3: Recirculation Mechanisms

**Supported by 19 fields** — Taba-to-Ba feedback loop

"Taba is not an endpoint but the seed of the next cycle's Ba"

**Five recirculation modes identified by evolutionary biology**:

1. **Natural return** — Taba directly becomes the next Ba
2. **Active return** — Taba actively modifies Ba
3. **Dramatic return** — A single Taba defines all subsequent Ba
4. **Unstable Taba** — Taba fails to stabilize and Uzu persists
5. **Branching Taba** — Taba does not converge to a single outcome and diverges

**Core insight**: Taba-to-Ba is not "cycling back to the same Ba" but an **irreversible spiral**.

## Theme 4: Multi-layered Nature of Ba

**Directly supported by 15 fields** — Ba is not "nothingness"

| Category | Character | Representative |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Primordial Ba | Structure does not yet exist | Astronomy (interstellar molecular clouds) |
| Cyclical Ba | Structured state created by previous Taba | Earth science, Evolutionary biology |

10 fields independently distinguished these two types.

**Core insight**: Ba is **not a passive precondition but an active structure**. Just as the quantum vacuum in physics is not "nothingness" but "a sea of fluctuations," Ba contains latent potential.

## Theme 5: Blind Spots and Counterexamples

**Pointed out by 13 fields** — An honest description of the model's limits

| Blind spot | Content |
|------------|---------|
| **Order dissolution** | The five stages describe generation but treat dissolution ambiguously |
| **Resistance to staging** | Rhizomatic thinking (philosophy), diversity in number of stages (religious studies) |
| **Implicit assumptions** | Temporality, involvement of consciousness, energy supply |

**"Destructive Uzu"**: Mass extinctions in Earth science are Uzu but not creative.

**Core insight**: The five-stage model is a descriptive framework for order generation and does not claim to encompass all change. This limitation is positioned as **descriptive honesty**.
