# T7+T9: Blind Spots and Counterexamples — Conclusion Summary

13 domains identify order-dissolution and anti-creative processes as blind spots; 11 domains suggest descriptive limits of the five-stage model.

- **The five stages specialize in "order generation" and lack a theory of "order dissolution"**: The most consistent finding across all 30 domains
- **Dissolution is not the "reverse" of the five stages**: Civilizational collapse and entropy increase do not proceed in the reverse order bundle-to-vortex-to-edge-to-wave-to-field
- **"Failure modes" are inherent to the five stages**: Non-arrival at vortex, bundle pathology, and edge misjudgment are stage-specific deviation patterns
- **"Planned dissolution" is part of generation**: Cases like apoptosis and disturbance management create new fields through dissolution

The five stages implicitly presuppose that order generation occurs, that cycling happens, that stages are distinguishable, scale neutrality, and value neutrality — each of which has counterexamples. The proposal adds a failure-mode taxonomy specific to each stage.
