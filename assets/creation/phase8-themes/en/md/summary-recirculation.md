# T5: Recirculation Mechanism — Conclusion Summary

19 of 30 domains explicitly reference bundle-to-field circulation (recirculation). Four convergence points were confirmed.

- **A bundle is not an endpoint but a seed for the next cycle's field**: The most robust proposition, converging across 15+ domains
- **Circulation is an irreversible spiral**: Not a "return to the original field" but a "transition to a new field altered by the bundle"
- **The driving force of circulation derives from the incompleteness of the bundle**: Bundles return to the field precisely because they leave something behind
- **Circulation has multiple modes**: Natural collapse, active renewal, dramatic transformation, institutional cycling, and intrinsic cycling

The definition of "bundle" should be updated from "a stabilized arrival point" to "a dynamic phase that prepares the next field." The five-stage diagram should shift from "line + arrow" to "spiral." A distinction between primordial field (type-0) and circulatory field (type-1) is necessary.
