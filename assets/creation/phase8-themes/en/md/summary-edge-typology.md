# T1: Edge Typology and Unified Classification — Conclusion Summary

![T1: Edge Typology and Unified Classification — Conclusion Summary](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/creation/img/svg/themes/en/theme-edge-typology.svg)

![T1 Edge Typology Convergence Network](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/creation/img/svg/themes/ja/theme-edge-typology-01-convergence-svg.svg)

28 of 30 domains thematize the polymorphism of "edge." Five convergent structures emerged from edge descriptions across all 30 domains.

- **Edge is a "band"**: Not a line but a region with width. The most robust cross-domain finding
- **Edge is a "directional selection site"**: Not merely an "in-between" but the place where outcomes are determined
- **Edge arises from "antagonism"**: A site where opposing forces meet is required
- **Edge is implementation-independent**: The essence is the structural feature of "selection through relationship," not any specific mechanism
- **Edge operates in two directions — binding and unbinding**: It works not only to "tie" but also to "untie"

Rather than merging typologies, a unified classification should aim to identify a small set of coordinate axes. The proposal defines edge as a space described by six coordinates: when / how / where / through what / on what / who.
