Blind Spot 2: Dissolution Is Not the "Reverse Order"

The dissolution process is not bundle → vortex → edge → wave → field in reverse.

  • Civilizational collapse does not follow the reverse of the five stages (history)
  • Entropy increase has its own structure (complex systems)
  • Chemical decomposition is not the reverse process of the five stages (chemistry)

Applying the five stages reversibly is inappropriate.

Planned Dissolution as Part of Generation

Not everything is a blind spot — planned dissolution is one form of bundle-to-field return.

  • Apoptosis: Cell death essential for development
  • Satoyama disturbance management: Intervention essential for stability maintenance
  • Architectural demolition → reconstruction: Creation of a new field

However, some dissolutions — like extinction — cannot return to a field.

Bifurcation Point: Expand the Scope or Acknowledge the Limits?

  • Option A: Expand the five stages to describe dissolution as well (destructive vortex, negative bundle)
  • Option B: Acknowledge the theory as specialized in order generation
  • Is staging essential or convenient?: "5" holds no privileged status
  • Value neutrality: Should cancer and lock-in both be called "bundle"?

Conclusion

The five stages are a theory specialized in "order generation," and order dissolution requires separate theorization.

That all 30 domains presented some blind spot is not a limitation of the model but an indicator of its soundness. The existence of failure modes specific to each stage enhances the theory's descriptive power.

Dissolution is not the "reverse" of the five stages; it possesses its own structure.
