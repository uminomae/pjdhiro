# Pharmacy: One Domain Contains the Full Spectrum of Edge, from Precision Design to Unintended Encounter

## 1. Purpose and Question of the Investigation

![Pharmacy: One Domain Contains the Full Spectrum of Edge, from Precision Design to Unintended Encounter — 調査概要インフォグラフィック](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/creation/img/svg/domains/ja/D11-01-overview-svg.svg)

This report presents the results of an investigation into the structural correspondence between the five-stage model of creation, Field -> Wave -> Edge -> Vortex -> Bundle, and major theories and practical systems in pharmacy.

Pharmacy is a practical discipline that manages processes across multiple scales, from molecular interaction to public-health systems. Finding a new drug, designing it, ensuring quality, and delivering it to patients are all part of one continuous chain. The aim of this investigation is to examine how the structure of the five-stage model appears across that chain.

This report is an investigation report. It is neither a textbook of pharmacy nor a document intended to prove the five-stage model correct. Cases where correspondence is strong and cases where it is weak are reported with the same standard.

## 2. Method of Investigation

This investigation selected ten theories and practical systems from pharmacy and evaluated their structural correspondence with the five-stage model.

**Selection criteria**: Candidates were chosen so as to cover the major branches of pharmacy, drug discovery, pharmacokinetics, formulation design, quality assurance, and traditional medicine, across scales from molecules to institutions.

**Method of evaluation**: Each candidate was examined for structural correspondence from multiple independent angles. Correspondence with Edge was evaluated in particular against three conditions: whether a relational network is present, whether indeterminacy is present, and whether the structure connects onward to Vortex.

**Criteria of judgment**: Cases were judged to show strong structural correspondence when all five stages had clear counterparts and all three Edge conditions were strongly present. Cases involving only partial or interpretive correspondence were treated as partial correspondence.

**Limits of the investigation**: Confidence ratings for the candidates tend to cluster at the same level, which limits their discriminating power. In addition, the quantitative literature supporting traditional East Asian medicine is still developing.

## 3. Overview of the Five-Stage Model

This section gives a brief outline of the five-stage model used in the investigation.

**Field** is an undifferentiated condition. It is the initial state in which neither direction nor structure has yet been fixed.

**Wave** is the stage of exploration in which multiple directions diverge and compete. Difference becomes visible and fluctuation appears.

**Edge** is the tense condition in which opposing elements coexist without converging into either side. Multiple relations intersect, and the situation cannot be resolved by a simple either-or. Whether one can remain at this stage bears strongly on the quality of creation.

**Vortex** is the stage in which a new coherence or order rises spontaneously from within the tension. A structure that did not previously exist emerges when intersecting relations reach a critical density.

**Bundle** is the stage in which the form is fixed and stabilizes as a reusable structure. What has emerged becomes the precondition for the next Field, and the cycle continues.

The five stages do not proceed only in a straight line. They circulate. The return from Bundle to Field, beginning the next round of exploration from what has already been formed, is what allows creation to continue.

Readers may already feel some discomfort with these definitions. That discomfort is directly connected to the question being tested here, and it may be worth carrying it forward while reading.

## 4. Results: Overall Picture

![Pharmacy: One Domain Contains the Full Spectrum of Edge, from Precision Design to Unintended Encounter — 理論×5段階対応マトリクス](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/creation/img/svg/domains/ja/D11-02-theories-map-svg.svg)

All ten candidates evaluated in the pharmacy domain showed some structural correspondence with the five-stage model. Six of them showed strong correspondence with Edge, meaning that all three Edge conditions, relational network, indeterminacy, and connection to Vortex, were strongly present. The remaining four showed partial correspondence. No candidate showed no correspondence at all.

This is a very high level even within the full thirty-domain investigation. It indicates that pharmacy is a domain especially rich in structural correspondence with the five-stage model.

The candidates fall into four clusters.

- **Institutional and management systems**: the drug-development pipeline and Quality by Design, or QbD (ICH Q8/Q9/Q10), centered on institutionalization
- **Dynamics and control systems**: pharmacokinetics (ADME-PK/PD) and oral sustained-release dosage forms, centered on feedback control
- **Relational and Edge systems**: Kampo prescription design (Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi), pharmacogenomics, and drug-drug interactions (DDI), centered on the diversity of Edge itself
- **Molecular design systems**: prodrug design and structure-based drug design (SBDD), centered on designing Edge at molecular scale

In addition to these, antimicrobial resistance evolution forms an independent case centered on the Bundle -> Field cycle.

## 5. Results: Findings by Theory

### 5-1. QbD in Formulation Development and Quality Assurance (ICH Q8/Q9/Q10)

ICH Q8/Q9/Q10 are international guidelines for pharmaceutical quality assurance. Q8(R2) is built on the idea that quality should not be tested into a product, but designed into it. It covers everything from setting a quality target product profile, QTPP, to constructing Design Space and establishing a Control Strategy. Q9 defines the risk-management cycle, and Q10 defines the framework of continual improvement.

In the official ICH definition, Design Space is described as the multidimensional combination and interaction of input variables and process parameters that have been shown to assure quality. The explicit appearance of interaction in that definition is important. It can be read as regulatory language describing the same structure as Edge in the five-stage model, a tense configuration in which multiple factors coexist without collapsing into one side.

Moreover, the operational freedom available within Design Space is a way of institutionally permitting indeterminacy. In terms of the five stages, QTPP corresponds to Field, knowledge amplification through development studies to Wave, Design Space to Edge, the risk cycle of Q9 and the continuous-improvement loop of Q10 to Vortex, and Control Strategy together with post-approval operation to Bundle. This is one of the strongest candidates in the entire domain, with clear correspondence across all five stages.

### 5-2. Kampo Prescription Design (Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi and Multi-Component Networks)

Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi is a traditional principle for designing Kampo formulas. It organizes the prescription in four layers: the sovereign component with the main therapeutic role, the minister that supports it, the assistant that moderates side effects or adjusts direction, and the envoy that harmonizes the whole. Work in network pharmacology has shown that this traditional design principle can be described quantitatively as a multi-compound, multi-target, multi-pathway network at molecular scale.

Compatibility, the way components are combined, treats relation itself as a design principle. What determines the quality of the prescription is not merely the efficacy of each component, but the structure of the relations among them. This is one of the most direct pharmaceutical implementations of Edge. It is also notable that molecular-scale networks and formula-level compatibility describe the same structure of relation at different scales.

In five-stage terms, the diagnostic pattern or therapeutic target corresponds to Field, the fluctuation introduced by the principal herb corresponds to Wave, complementary compatibility among ingredients corresponds to Edge, simultaneous action across multiple compounds, targets, and pathways corresponds to Vortex, and fixation as a formula corresponds to Bundle.

### 5-3. Evolution of Antimicrobial Resistance

Antimicrobial resistance evolution is the process by which bacteria carrying resistance mutations spread and become fixed under the selection pressure of antimicrobial agents. Through horizontal transfer, including plasmids, resistance genes move across species. Fleming warned of this possibility already in his 1945 Nobel lecture, and antimicrobial resistance has since become a central public-health issue.

What stands out most in its correspondence with the five-stage model is the clarity of the Bundle -> Field cycle. The development and approval of a new drug, Bundle, creates a new problem in the form of resistant bacteria, which becomes the next Field. The structure in which one solution produces the next problem operates simultaneously at three scales: molecular mutation on short time scales, cellular and population-level spread of resistance on yearly scales, and revision of treatment standards and policy over decades.

The fact that intentional human intervention, antimicrobial use, produces the unintended consequence of resistance evolution shows concretely that the Bundle -> Field cycle in the five-stage model does not proceed according to plan. A gap can open between prediction and reality.

### 5-4. Pharmacokinetics (ADME-PK/PD) and Dose Optimization

ADME-PK/PD is the framework for quantitatively describing absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion, and combining these with pharmacodynamics to design optimal dosing schedules. Compartment models predict blood-concentration trajectories, while genetic polymorphisms in CYP enzymes shape individual differences in metabolic rate.

In five-stage terms, the pre-dose homeostatic condition corresponds to Field, the fluctuation in blood concentration after administration corresponds to Wave, the many-actor network of receptors, enzymes, and transporters, including individual variation caused by CYP polymorphisms, corresponds to Edge, the dynamic balance between therapeutic effect and adverse effect corresponds to Vortex, and establishment of an optimal dosing regimen corresponds to Bundle.

A distinctive trait of this framework is that each stage can be expressed mathematically. A control structure is present in which dose is the manipulated variable, blood concentration is the controlled variable, and the therapeutic window functions as the set point. From molecular binding constants to organism-level concentration curves, the same feedback structure repeats across different scales.

### 5-5. Drug-Drug Interactions (DDI)

Drug-drug interactions, DDI, are kinetic changes that occur when multiple drugs are used together and CYP enzymes are inhibited or induced. The number of possible combinations is enormous, and it is impossible in principle to map the full space exhaustively in advance. For this reason, regulatory guidance has increasingly demanded systematic evaluation.

DDI is a classic case of unintended encounter. In five-stage terms, the state of polypharmacy corresponds to Field, interference among metabolic pathways to Wave, unexpected interaction through CYP inhibition or induction to Edge, emergent combined effects to Vortex, and guideline-based contraindications or dose adjustments to Bundle.

The complexity of the relational network, the indeterminacy created by combinatorial explosion, and the strong connection to Vortex are all present at high intensity. DDI is therefore one of the strongest Edge candidates in this domain. Within the Edge spectrum discussed below, it sits at the end where control is most difficult.

### 5-6. Other Candidates

**The drug-development pipeline** narrows the search space step by step from target discovery to post-approval surveillance. Go/No-Go judgments at each stage are institutionalized as regulatory science. The stage structure corresponds clearly to the five stages, but its core is selection rather than generation, which makes it somewhat different in character from other creation structures.

**Pharmacogenomics** provides the biological basis for individualized Edge. Genetic polymorphisms in CYP enzymes, often summarized as PM, IM, EM, and UM categories, determine metabolic rate. The fact that the metabolic pattern remains unsettled until genotype is known is a case in which the indeterminacy of Edge is materially grounded in genetic information.

**Oral sustained-release formulation design** quantitatively describes drug release through the Higuchi equation, Q = K sqrt(t). It is a pharmaceutical version of a diffusion equation, and is an objective candidate in the sense that the Wave stage can be tracked mathematically. At the same time, the connection between engineering design and creation remains partly interpretive.

**Prodrug design** administers an inactive precursor produced by chemical modification, which is then converted into the active form by enzymes in the body. Encounter with a bodily enzyme is the key to activation, giving the structure the form of building the intervention of Edge directly into the design.

**Structure-based drug design (SBDD)** determines the three-dimensional structure of a target protein and designs molecules that fit its binding site. The stereochemical complementarity between ligand and protein is one of the most precisely controlled examples of Edge at molecular scale.

## 6. Cross-Domain Patterns

![Pharmacy: One Domain Contains the Full Spectrum of Edge, from Precision Design to Unintended Encounter — 横断的パターン図](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/creation/img/svg/domains/ja/D11-03-cross-patterns-svg.svg)

### The Edge Spectrum: A Gradient of Controllability

The most important contribution of pharmacy to the broader investigation is the Edge spectrum.

At one end is atomic-scale stereochemical complementarity in structure-based drug design, the most precise form of design. Next comes encounter with bodily enzymes in prodrug activation, a planned triggering structure. Then come individual differences caused by CYP polymorphisms in pharmacokinetics and pharmacogenomics, where control becomes partial and individualized. At the far end are drug-drug interactions, where combinatorial explosion creates unintended encounter.

Along this sequence, the controllability of Edge declines step by step. From precise design to unpredictable encounter, the full spectrum of Edge exists within a single domain. That is a distinctive feature of pharmacy.

This framework may also help analyze Edge in other domains. By asking where a given Edge lies on this spectrum, one may gain a more precise understanding of the concept itself.

### Institutionalization of Relational Structure

A second distinctive trait of pharmacy is the development of systems that manage relation itself at the level of principle and institution. The Design Space of ICH Q8 defines interaction space as a regulatory object. DDI guidance systematizes evaluation of interaction among drugs. Kampo compatibility treats relation among ingredients as a design principle. In each case, what is being managed is not simply the property of each element in isolation, but the structure of relations among elements.

### Fractal Structure Across Scales

The same pattern of relational structure appears repeatedly from atomic scale to public-health scale: molecular interaction in the binding pocket of structure-based drug design; enzyme-level individual difference in CYP polymorphism; prescription-level compatibility in Kampo formulation; institutional quality assurance in ICH Q8/Q9/Q10; and public-health systems for antimicrobial resistance.

The same is true of time scale, from minutes in drug metabolism to decades in the development pipeline or resistance evolution. The observed structural correspondence is not confined to a single scale.

## 7. Unresolved Questions

- **Tension between design and creation**: Formulation design, QbD, and structure-based drug design place intentional control at the center. This creates a tension with the five-stage model's emphasis on spontaneous emergence of order. The tension is visible, but not resolved.
- **Connecting East Asian medicine and quantitative science**: Kampo compatibility and network pharmacology describe relational wholeness in different languages. How far traditional diagnostic patterns and modern quantitative analysis correspond structurally remains insufficiently supported.
- **Overlap and separation among candidates**: Pharmacokinetics, pharmacogenomics, and drug-drug interactions partly overlap. Their distinction as dynamic model, genetic source of individual difference, and unintended interaction seems defensible, but it still deserves further examination.
- **Discriminating power of confidence ratings**: All ten candidates cluster at nearly the same confidence level. Finer criteria, such as whether cited literature has been independently verified or whether numerical claims have been recalculated, remain a task for future refinement.

## 8. Conclusion

Pharmacy is a domain with very high structural correspondence to the five-stage model. All ten candidates showed correspondence, and six of them showed strong Edge correspondence.

Two findings stand out most clearly.

First is the Edge spectrum. From precision design to unintended encounter, the gradient of controllability is fully present within a single domain. This is an important finding not only for pharmacy, but for the thirty-domain investigation as a whole, because it reveals the many-sided character of Edge.

Second is the institutionalization of relation. As seen in the Design Space of ICH Q8 and the compatibility structures of Kampo, pharmacy has developed systems that treat relation itself as an object of principle and institutional management. This domain provides abundant real examples of Edge being operated as an institution.

At the same time, caution is still required in interpretation. The tension between candidates centered on intentional control and the five-stage model's premise of spontaneous emergence does not invalidate structural correspondence, but neither has it been resolved. The strength of correspondence can be reported with confidence, while the meaning of that correspondence remains partly a matter for further examination.
