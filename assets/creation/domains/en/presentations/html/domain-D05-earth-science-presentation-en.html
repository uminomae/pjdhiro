Earth Science

Structural correspondence survey with the 5-stage model (Field, Wave, Edge, Vortex, Bundle)

Survey Overview

  • Subjects examined: 14 major theories in earth science
  • Research question: Do the various theories of earth science correspond structurally to the 5-stage model?
  • Findings: 7 strong correspondences, 3 conditional correspondences

Overview of the 5-Stage Model

Key Entry 1: Planetary Differentiation

  • As established fact, the proto-Earth is thought to have undergone large-scale melting driven by accretionary energy and heat from short-lived radioactive nuclides, passing through a magma ocean stage (Elkins-Tanton 2012). Iron-nickel alloy then gravitationally separated from silicate to form the core, and the Hf-W isotope system shows this differentiation was largely complete within tens of millions of years after Earth's formation (Kleine et al. 2002). Furthermore, repeated solidification of the magma ocean and igneous activity led to mantle stratification and long-term crustal growth (Solomatov 2000; Taylor and McLennan 1985).
  • As a reading, what is important here is that a homogeneous, mixed state transitions irreversibly to a stratified structure through the coupled conditions of heat, composition, and density. The level of analogy is structure. The Edge in particular can be read as the phase in which the immiscibility of iron and silicate begins to operate but it is not yet determined whether separation will occur. The existence of bodies like the Moon that remain incompletely differentiated shows that the Edge is not simply a boundary name but a set of conditions for a fork in fate.
  • As an interpretation, in planetary differentiation, the 5 stages are not cycling within an already-existing Field but are creating the very foundation upon which the entire subsequent Earth system stands. The Edge here is not merely a boundary but the generative condition that determines the range of motion for all subsequent processes.
  • As a mapping, the Field corresponds to the mixed state of the protoplanetary body, the Wave to the progression of heating and melting, the Edge to the onset conditions for separation of the metallic and silicate phases, the Vortex to the mutual reinforcement of iron sinking and heating, and the Bundle to the stratified structure of core, mantle, and crust.
  • As an implication, planetary differentiation teaches us that the 5 stages do not merely describe changes occurring on top of a Field but can also create that Field itself. Subsequent plate motion, magnetic field generation, and volcanic activity are all possible only on top of this Bundle.
  • As a limitation, planetary differentiation is a one-time event and relies substantially on geochemical reconstruction rather than direct observation. Therefore, even if the trajectory from Field to Bundle is clear, the passage through the Edge cannot be traced in temporal sequence directly.

Key Entry 2: Plate Tectonics and the Wilson Cycle

  • As established fact, the lithosphere is divided into multiple plates that move through the combined driving forces of mantle convection, slab pull, and ridge push (Morgan 1968; Le Pichon 1968). The resulting Wilson Cycle—continental rifting → seafloor spreading → subduction → ocean closure → continental collision → supercontinent formation—has been proposed to repeat on timescales of hundreds of millions of years, with Rodinia and Pangaea as representative examples (Wilson 1966; Dewey and Bird 1970). Aulacogens—failed rifts—are the record of rifting that began but did not progress to seafloor spreading.
  • As a reading, the backbone of this theory lies in the accumulation of fluctuations after long periods of stability, followed by passage through a branch point where the rift either succeeds or is repaired, leading to the onset of self-sustaining spreading and subduction, and ultimately fixation as a large-scale configuration. The level of analogy is intermediate between structure and process. The Edge in particular is the phase in which it remains long undetermined whether a continental rift will succeed or end as a failed rift, and where heat flow, stress, and crustal weaknesses assemble as a set of relationships.
  • As an interpretation, in plate tectonics, the Edge does not operate as a single cut-point but as a hinge that advances the cycle to the next phase. This makes it clear that the second half of the 5 stages is not a terminus but includes a turning back toward the next Field.
  • As a mapping, the Field corresponds to the mantle-lithosphere system during periods of supercontinent stability, the Wave to rift precursors from plumes or thermal disturbances, the Edge to the bifurcation between a successful rift and a failed rift, the Vortex to the self-sustaining loop in which seafloor spreading and subduction mutually reinforce each other, and the Bundle to the configuration of supercontinents or ocean basins.
  • As an implication, plate tectonics shows that the 5 stages can cycle at even the largest scales. It is also a theory that demonstrates, at the level of Earth history, that the Bundle is not a terminus but folds back into the next Field.
  • As a limitation, the relative contributions to plate driving forces remain debated, and the driving of the Vortex cannot be reduced to a single mechanism. Therefore, even if the correspondence with the 5 stages is clear, explaining each stage through a single cause is unreasonable.

Key Entry 3: ENSO

  • As established fact, in the tropical Pacific, sea surface temperature anomalies, trade winds, upwelling, and the thermocline are coupled, giving rise to El Niño and La Niña at irregular intervals of roughly 2 to 10 years (Philander 1983). Bjerknes showed that sea surface temperature anomalies generate wind stress anomalies, which in turn amplify sea surface temperature anomalies through ocean response—a positive feedback (Bjerknes 1969). The coupled model of Cane and Zebiak (1985) and the delayed oscillator of Suarez and Schopf (1988) reproduce this positive feedback and phase reversal.
  • As a reading, what can be read here is the structure in which fluctuations transform into self-sustaining events only when multiple independent variables become linked. The level of analogy is mechanism. The Edge is the phase in which sea surface temperature, atmospheric heating, wind stress, and ocean waves have not yet converged into a relational network—rather than single factors—and where it is still undetermined whether Bjerknes feedback will actually activate.
  • As an interpretation, in ENSO, the Edge behaves less as a boundary line and more as a condition for the establishment of coupling. Whether a single perturbation transforms into an event depends on whether the elements have become connected, and it is here that the relational character of the 5-stage Edge is most clearly visible.
  • As a mapping, the Field corresponds to the normal Walker Circulation and mean ocean state, the Wave to initial perturbations from westerly wind bursts, Kelvin waves, and Rossby waves, the Edge to the coupling point of SST, atmosphere, and ocean dynamics, the Vortex to the amplification of the event through positive feedback, and the Bundle to the completed global pattern as El Niño or La Niña.
  • As an implication, ENSO most clearly demonstrates that the Edge cannot exist as a single threshold but only as a network of relationships. It is one of the most compelling cases in earth science for understanding the Edge of the 5-stage model not as a boundary line but as a coupled interface.
  • As a limitation, ENSO has large irregularity and inter-event diversity; each event does not proceed through the same sequence. Therefore, although the correspondence with the 5 stages is strong, fixing it too rigidly as a single temporal template will cause it to lose explanatory power.

Cross-Cutting Patterns

  • In ENSO, sea surface temperature, wind stress, and ocean waves become linked; in earthquakes, stress, friction, and pore water pressure intertwine; in planetary differentiation, temperature, pressure, composition, and density all operate simultaneously
  • Failed rifts, geomagnetic excursions that did not complete a reversal, and magma that solidified as intrusive rock without erupting are all examples of "advancing through the Wave but not clearing the Edge"
  • In ENSO and earthquakes, the Vortex appears as rapid amplification through positive feedback
  • In earth science, the Bundle remains as strata, magnetization, continental configurations, focal mechanisms, and volcanic deposits

Open Questions

  • For gradual transformations such as weathering and soil formation, it remains unresolved whether the Edge should be defined as a threshold or redefined as a thick reaction interface.
  • The 100-kyr problem in Milankovitch cycles has not yet determined whether the Edge is a threshold internal to the ice sheet or resides within the entire coupled system including orbit, ocean, and atmosphere.
  • How far a single definition can unify the destructive Vortex shown by mass extinctions and the organizing Vortex shown by ENSO and earthquakes is deferred.
  • How to position within the 5 stages an Edge whose temporal width changes depending on observational position—such as bounding surfaces in sequence stratigraphy—requires further clarification.

Conclusion

  • This survey confirmed that earth science is a domain with very strong structural similarity to the 5-stage model
  • At the same time, not all correspondences are of equal strength
