# Track 9A — Academic Grounding: Literature Alignment Study

## Purpose

Systematically investigate how well the structural themes extracted in Phase 8 align with existing academic scholarship. The goal is to clearly separate well-supported claims from untested ones, and to identify where genuine novelty exists.

## Initial Target

Starting with **Edge Typology**, the most broadly observed theme (28 of 30 domains).

| Order | Theme | Domains |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Edge Typology | 28 |
| 2 | Bundle-to-Field Recirculation | 19 |
| 3 | Field Multi-Layering | 15 |
| 4 | Threshold Structure | 11 |
| 5 | Blind Spots and Counterexamples | 24 |

## Methodology

### Step 1: Hypothesis Extraction

Decompose each theme's major claims into testable propositions.

Example: "Boundary condition types converge to four patterns across domains."

### Step 2: Literature Survey

For each proposition, identify relevant academic fields and search for prior research. Cross-disciplinary awareness is essential — the same phenomenon may be studied under different names in different fields. Meta-analyses and review papers take priority.

### Step 3: Alignment Analysis

Compare creation-space claims against academic findings using four categories:

| Judgment | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Supported | Prior research corroborates the claim |
| Partial | Conditionally or partially aligned |
| Contradicted | Prior research presents opposing evidence |
| Novel | No relevant prior research found |

### Step 4: Revision and Deepening

- Contradictions found: Revise the claim or add conditions
- Novel territory: Record as a novelty claim and derive testable predictions
- New questions emerge: Return to Step 1

## Deliverables

For each theme:

- **Proposition list** — Claims decomposed into testable form
- **Literature map** — Organized collection of discovered academic references
- **Alignment report** — Judgment results across four categories
- **Revised claims** — Updated versions reflecting findings (where applicable)
- **Prediction list** — Testable predictions for novel claims

## Convergence Criteria

- 80% or more of major propositions have received a judgment
- Five or more loops completed with no new discoveries
- A fundamental contradiction necessitating theme restructuring is identified
