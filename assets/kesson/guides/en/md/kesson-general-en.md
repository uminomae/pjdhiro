## 1. What Is Kesson-Driven Thinking?

Kesson-Driven Thinking (欠損駆動思考) is a way of thinking that retains the gap between expectation and reality — rather than discarding it — and holds it as a question that can become the starting point for creation.

This framework is not a specific methodology or technique. It describes, through four concepts, the quietly unsettling moments we tend to brush aside in daily life — what that feeling actually is, why we let it go so quickly, and what it takes to hold onto it.

## 2. Core Structure

Kesson-Driven Thinking is composed of four concepts.

| Concept | Definition |
|---|---|
| Kesson (欠損) | The subjective experience of feeling a gap between expectation and reality as a "missing piece" |
| Withhold | The function of holding Kesson as an open question rather than reflexively resolving it |
| Two Signals | The simultaneous pull of "resolve this quickly" and the quieter draw of "stay with this a little longer" |
| Container | The relational foundation that supports Withhold — including internalized relationships |

Each concept is explained below.

### Kesson (欠損)

Kesson is the subjective experience of consciousness registering the gap between expectation and reality as a "missing piece."

For example: you encounter something and feel *this isn't right*. You hear an explanation and something catches. In that moment, there is a mismatch between what your mind expected and what actually happened. When that mismatch surfaces in awareness as a sense of *something is missing* or *something is not here* — that is Kesson.

Kesson is not a flaw. It is not a prediction error. It is the experience of receiving a gap as a felt sense in your own body. If you process it as "my expectation was simply wrong," the Kesson disappears. Receiving the gap as "there is still something I cannot yet see" is the prerequisite for Withhold, described next.

### Withhold

When we notice something catching, the usual response is to resolve it quickly. We reach a conclusion — "it's probably this" — or we let it drift by. Withhold refers to not doing that: holding Kesson as an open question rather than immediately resolving it.

Withhold is not endurance. It is not giving up on finding an answer. It is the function of maintaining the state: "there is still something I cannot see in this catching feeling."

### Two Signals

When Kesson is felt, two signals arise simultaneously.

One is the signal "resolve this quickly." The body, having detected a mismatch, becomes unsettled and moves toward an answer to restore stability. This signal is strong and fast.

The other is the draw "stay with this a little longer." A sense that something is still unseen — that you would like to remain with this question a moment more. This draw is weak, slow, and easily overwhelmed by the first signal.

Neither signal is better than the other. The "resolve this quickly" signal is necessary for avoiding danger and acting efficiently. The "stay a little longer" draw is the starting point from which something new can emerge. Withhold is the stance of standing between these two signals.

### Container

Holding onto a catching feeling without reaching for an answer is extremely difficult to do alone. While the "resolve this quickly" signal rings loudly, maintaining Withhold requires support.

Container refers to the structure of relationships that provides that support. The "relationships" meant here are not limited to someone physically present. They include relationships internalized in the mind — the memory of a mentor who has passed, experiences with a caregiver in early childhood, the voice of someone who once said "you're okay."

When a trusted relationship exists inside, Withhold becomes easier to maintain. Conversely, when Container is absent, Withhold tends to collapse. The structure of "unable to wait alone" means that Withhold is a function that exists not through individual willpower but through relationship.

## 3. Why We Resolve So Quickly

The "resolve this quickly" signal is structurally strong.

This signal is directly connected to survival. Responding immediately to danger, restoring stability by resolving uncertainty — these are functions necessary for living. As a result, this signal is fast, strong, and mobilizes the whole body.

The "stay a little longer" draw, by contrast, is weak and slow. It is hard to put into words, and carries no urgency. In daily life, deadlines, efficiency, and pressure toward "reaching a conclusion" are always present. In this environment, the weaker draw is easily swallowed.

There are two main conditions under which Withhold is crushed.

The first is when the pressure of the "resolve this quickly" signal is too strong. Intense anxiety, deadlines, the surrounding pressure of "so, what are you going to do?" — these strip away the capacity to maintain Withhold.

The second is when Container is absent. Without a relational foundation that supports holding onto a catching feeling, maintaining Withhold alone is very difficult.

## 4. What It Is Useful For

What Kesson-Driven Thinking provides is not a solution, but an understanding of structure.

First, you become able to observe which signal is stronger right now. When you feel something catching, you can identify whether you are following the "resolve this quickly" signal or responding to the "stay a little longer" draw. This identification itself creates room to not immediately resolve.

Second, the conditions for Withhold become visible. You can understand "why did I let go of this catching feeling just now?" not as a failure of willpower, but as a matter of structure — the strength of the signal, the presence or absence of Container.

Third, the importance of Container becomes clear. The capacity to hold onto a catching feeling is not something cultivated alone through effort; it grows through relationship. You gain a lens for thinking about what kinds of relationships support Withhold.

## 5. Evidence and Limits

The current state of the evidence for this framework is presented honestly.

**What is considered certain**: The four concepts of Kesson-Driven Thinking — Kesson, Withhold, Two Signals, Container — are structures consistently confirmed in the exploration of this framework's originator. The definitions of these concepts are currently stable.

**What is promising but still under investigation**: The structure of the Two Signals has analogous descriptions across multiple academic fields. The structural connection between "holding Kesson" and "support through relationship" appears to point in the same direction as findings from psychoanalysis and developmental psychology. However, these correspondences require further verification.

**What is not yet understood**: The full picture of conditions under which Withhold succeeds is still being organized. The detailed process by which Container is internalized also requires further refinement. The range of situations in which this framework is effective, and where it is difficult to apply, also remains a future task.
