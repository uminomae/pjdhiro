# Using the Awareness Model as an Observation Tool

## Purpose

This guide explains the awareness model as a tool for observation in education, support, and team settings.
It is not a practice manual and it is not a diagnostic system.
Its role is to provide a structured way to read reactions without reducing them to personality, motivation, or ability alone.

## Executive Summary

- The model reads reaction as a layered process rather than as a single emotional label.
- The survival-trust axis helps distinguish whether a reaction is oriented mainly toward safety or toward relationship.
- The model may be practically useful, but it should still be treated as a developing framework rather than a settled method.

## Model Overview

![Survival-Trust Axis overview](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/awareness/img/svg/topics/en/survival-trust-axis-01-overview-svg.svg)

![Four-Layer Model overview](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/awareness/img/svg/topics/en/four-layers-01-overview-svg.svg)

The model reads awareness as a four-layer process.

- Layer 0: bodily state
- Layer 1: discrepancy between prediction and reality
- Layer 2: what kind of problem that discrepancy is taken to be
- Layer 3: room to revise response rather than fixing it immediately

The main interpretive hypothesis added to this process is the survival-trust axis.

- Survival axis: safety, defense, position, failure avoidance
- Trust axis: trust, relationship, being understood, belonging

A single silence, for example, may reflect an effort to avoid failure or an effort to preserve relationship.
How we observe it, and how we respond to it, changes depending on which reading is stronger.

## Research Basis and Reference Fields

This model is not derived from field intuition alone.
Its main reference fields are neurophenomenology and psychology.

From neurophenomenology, the project borrows questions about how first-person experience can be described.
At the moment, that means embodiment, foregrounding of discrepancy, first-person descriptive units, and prereflective awareness.
From psychology, it draws on attachment, development, trust, and intersubjectivity to examine the relational side of response.
More specifically, it is organizing attachment and safe base, internal working models, trust and intersubjectivity, and repairability as the main relational bundles.

## Current View

In practice, the model is useful because it slows down premature judgment.
Instead of moving immediately from reaction to evaluation, it asks what is happening at each layer.

### Education

When learning appears to stop, the first explanation is often lack of understanding.
This model asks whether bodily tension, discrepancy overload, or relationship anxiety is driving the situation instead.

### Support

In support settings, a strong reaction is often summarized as anxiety.
This model asks whether the anxiety is centered on safety or on relationship, because that changes the stance of support.

### Team Settings

In teams, personal response and group response often amplify each other.
A defensive reaction in one person may return as relationship anxiety in another and increase tension across the group.
The model can help track that chain.

## Key Limitations and Next Questions

At present, the layered reading of bodily state, discrepancy, orientation, and response flexibility looks promising.
However, the model is still being organized as a coherent framework.

The main open questions are:

- How clearly can survival-oriented and relationship-oriented problems be separated?
- How should neurophenomenology and psychology divide explanatory roles?
- How far can bodily strain and discrepancy salience be distinguished in observation?
- How far can relational safety and repairability be treated as observational guides?
- How can this observational framework be made more testable?

The model may already be useful in practice, but that does not mean it has become a finalized method.
