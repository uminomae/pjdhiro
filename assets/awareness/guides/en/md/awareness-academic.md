# Awareness and Intersubjectivity — Circumstantial Evidence from Neurophenomenology, Psychology, and Philosophy

## Purpose of This Document

This document is an exploratory text examining the place of intersubjectivity within awareness from the perspectives of multiple academic disciplines. Its aim is not to declare a finished theory but to distinguish among established findings, circumstantial evidence, and hypotheses, and to present each category clearly.

The intended audience is interdisciplinary explorers with interests in neurophenomenology, cognitive science, developmental psychology, clinical psychology, or philosophy.

## Summary

- The core hypothesis of this exploration is that signals related to intersubjectivity are processed by the nervous system with roughly the same weight as survival-related signals.
- This hypothesis is supported by circumstantial evidence spanning multiple levels of evidence — from the molecular to the social and historical — but has not been directly tested by any single study.
- Philosophical and clinical traditions are positioned not as "evidence" but as "perspectives that point to the same intersection from a different angle."

## 1. Established Findings

### 1.1 Interoception and the Foundations of Awareness

Craig (2002) identified the afferent system that transmits every aspect of the body's physiological state to the brain. This interoceptive system constitutes a representation of "the material self" and provides the substrate of subjective feeling.

Damasio's (1994) somatic marker hypothesis demonstrated that bodily sensations accompanying emotion guide decision-making. Damage to the ventromedial prefrontal cortex produces severe impairments in decision-making while leaving intellectual capacity intact.

These represent robust findings establishing that awareness is grounded in bodily signals.

### 1.2 Predictive Coding and the Free Energy Principle

Friston (2010) formulated the brain's basic operating principle as the minimization of prediction error, providing a theoretical framework that accounts for perception, action, and learning in a unified manner.

> **LLM note**: Clark (2013) elaborated Friston's mathematical framework in the language of cognitive science (predictive processing). As the author has not been able to verify this understanding independently, it is noted here for reference rather than incorporated into the main argument.

### 1.3 The Debate on the Construction of Emotion

Barrett's (2017) constructionist theory of emotion argues that emotions are the result of the brain constructing experience through prediction, interoception, and conceptualization. Emotion categories are not natural kinds but constructed entities.

Panksepp (1998) argues for the evolutionary conservation of seven primary emotional systems (SEEKING, RAGE, FEAR, LUST, CARE, PANIC/GRIEF, PLAY).

Van Heijst et al. (2025) described this debate as "irreconcilable but complementary." Barrett addresses proximate mechanisms (how); Panksepp addresses ultimate causes (why). **The core hypothesis advanced here is compatible with either position.**

## 2. The Core Hypothesis and Circumstantial Evidence

### 2.1 Core Hypothesis

> Among the cognitive signals that rise from the nervous system to awareness, signals related to intersubjectivity (trust) are processed with roughly the same weight as survival-related signals.

This is an integrative claim located at the intersection of multiple prior studies; no single study has directly tested it. The following presents circumstantial evidence spanning multiple levels of analysis. No single piece of evidence proves the core hypothesis, but together — like the blind scholars and the elephant — they point to the same intersection from different angles.

### 2.2 Multi-Level Circumstantial Evidence

#### Molecular Level

**Cole (2007)**: Differential expression of 209 genes in leukocytes of lonely individuals. Upregulation of pro-inflammatory genes (NF-kB), downregulation of antiviral genes (IRF) = CTRA (conserved transcriptional response to adversity). Replicated cross-sectionally across 11 conditions (bereavement, low SES, PTSD, etc.) in subsequent studies.

**Limitation**: Initial study N=14. Correlational design; causal direction requires caution.

#### Endocrine Level

**Smith & Wang (2014)**: Demonstrated that PVN oxytocin in prairie voles causally mediates social buffering.

**Limitation**: Causal demonstration in animal models is robust, but human intranasal administration studies have serious replication problems (Nave et al. 2015, Walum et al. 2016).

**Honest assessment**: The direction of the conclusion is supported, but the basis for extrapolation to humans is weak. This distinction is explicitly noted.

#### Neural Circuit Level

**Eisenberger et al. (2003)**: Activation of the dACC during social exclusion (N=13, Cyberball paradigm).

**dACC debate**: Wager et al. (2016) interpret this as general salience/conflict detection. Woo et al. (2014) found pattern-level separation using MVPA (classification accuracy 92%).

**Current consensus**: "Distinct signals that partially share the same urgency channel."

**Panksepp (1998)**: The PANIC/GRIEF system — separation distress vocalizations, circuitry including PAG, ACC, and BNST, modulated by endogenous opioids, OXT, and prolactin. Even within Barrett's constructionist interpretation, the survival relevance of intersubjective signals is not in dispute.

#### Physiological/Behavioral Level

**Coan & Sbarra (2015)**: Social Baseline Theory. The brain presupposes proximity to social relationships as its default. Social resources are described as "bioenergetic resources equivalent to oxygen or glucose."

Coan et al. (2006): Holding a spouse's hand attenuates threat-related neural activity. The "attenuation" of threat responses is interpreted as a return to the true baseline.

**Relationship to core hypothesis**: SBT contains claims that are even stronger than the core hypothesis. The core hypothesis is positioned as a conservative lower bound of SBT.

#### Psychological Level

**Baumeister & Leary (1995)**: The need to belong satisfies all nine criteria for designation as a fundamental motive (universality, emotional consequences, orientation of cognitive processing, health costs when thwarted, satiation model, etc.). Over 30,000 citations.

The satiation model (quality over quantity, motivational decline after threshold is crossed) is structurally parallel to physiological homeostasis.

#### Epidemiological Level

**Holt-Lunstad et al. (2010)**: Meta-analysis of 148 studies, 308,849 participants. Odds ratio for survival among those with richer social relationships: OR=1.50.

**Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015)**: 70 studies, 3.4 million individuals. Loneliness OR=1.26, social isolation OR=1.29, living alone OR=1.32.

Probabilistic in nature; variance due to intensity and resilience variables. Treated as circumstantial evidence.

#### Evolutionary Level

**Dunbar (1998)**: The social brain hypothesis. Correlation between neocortex ratio and social group size in primates R²=0.76. The field has moved from a single-factor account toward a multi-factor co-evolutionary model, but the significant contribution of social factors is widely recognized.

#### Social/Historical Level

**Durkheim (1897)**: Diminished social integration elevates suicide rates. The core proposition has been sustained across 130 years of replication.

### 2.3 Evaluation of the Evidence

**Most robust**: Holt-Lunstad meta-analysis (3.4 million individuals), Baumeister & Leary (systematic examination across nine criteria), Durkheim's accumulated epidemiology.

**Circumstantial evidence for causal mechanisms**: Cole CTRA (molecular pathway), Smith & Wang (OT pathway, animal model), Coan hand-holding experiment (changes in neural activity).

**Contested**: Interpretation of dACC activation, Panksepp vs. Barrett on emotion categories, precision of Dunbar's number, efficacy of OT administration in humans.

**Falsifiability**: The core hypothesis would be falsified if any of the following were demonstrated:
- A cognitive signal is found that is prioritized more strongly than either survival or trust (intersubjectivity)
- A category of information other than survival and trust is shown to be processed with highest priority in human awareness and mind

**Current falsification status**: A survey across 30 fields has not identified any study that directly falsifies the core hypothesis.

## 3. Philosophical and Clinical Foundations

Whereas Section 2 assembled circumstantial evidence from multiple empirical fields, this section presents bodies of thought that address conceptually why intersubjectivity is constitutive. These are not evidence; they are perspectives pointing to the same intersection from a different angle.

The level of abstraction engaged here is existential and phenomenological; the discussion is not reduced to the level of psychology or cognitive science.

### 3.1 Three Thinkers Closest to the Core

**Merleau-Ponty (intercorporeality)**: Situated intersubjectivity at a pre-reflective, bodily dimension. The other's body appears directly in the perceptual field as "another self." Awareness does not "infer" the other; the body pre-reflectively intertwines with the other. This is directionally consistent with the neuroscientific evidence in Section 2.

**Stolorow (intersubjectivity and trauma)**: "The isolated mind is a myth." Emotional experience is always organized within an intersubjective context. Trauma's essence lies not in the event itself but in the intersubjective breakdown of emotional attunement. This stops short of claiming that all trauma is intersubjective breakdown.

**Nagarjuna / Heart Sutra / Quantum Field Theory (non-duality)**: Nagarjuna's dependent origination — no independently self-subsisting entity exists; all existence arises through mutual dependence. The Heart Sutra's "form is emptiness." Quantum field theory — particles are excitation states of a field, not independent entities. These three traditions, from different lineages, say the same thing: **there are no independent entities; everything arises from relationship.** The two-axis framework of "survival vs. intersubjectivity" in the core hypothesis is operationally useful as a distinction, but the logic of dependent origination suggests that this binary opposition is ultimately inseparable.

### 3.3 Positioning as Antithesis

What Sections 2 and 3 together demonstrate is that intersubjectivity is not "second only to money in importance" but matters at a level equivalent to life itself.

This is an antithesis to capitalist economics — the framework that assigns highest priority to material and economic value. It is not, however, a negation. Economic rationality is also indispensable for sustaining and developing life, and survival and intersubjectivity stand in an inclusive relationship, like the interplay of yin and yang. What this exploration claims is the inversion of a hierarchy that places intersubjectivity beneath economic value, and the repositioning of both as co-equal foundational dimensions.
