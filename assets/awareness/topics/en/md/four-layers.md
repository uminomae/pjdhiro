# Report on the Four-Layer Model

## 1. Purpose of This Document

![Four-Layer Model overview](https://uminomae.github.io/pjdhiro/assets/awareness/img/svg/topics/en/four-layers-01-overview-svg.svg)

This document explains a way of describing awareness not as a static collection of contents, but as a process.
It organizes that minimal structure as a chain composed of four layers.

## 2. Structure of the Model

Layer 0 concerns the bodily basis of awareness, including respiration, tension, and heartbeat.
The main point is that awareness does not begin as abstract thought alone, but rises from bodily state.
What matters here is that Layer 0 is not yet making problem judgments.
It provides the bodily footing of first-person experience.

Layer 1 concerns the comparison between prediction and reality.
Not every discrepancy becomes distress or conflict in the same way; at this layer, discrepancy is detected and selected.
Many discrepancies are absorbed prereflectively, while only some remain as the beginnings of unease or lack.

Layer 2 concerns the direction in which that discrepancy is interpreted as a problem.
Whether it is read as a survival problem or as a trust-and-relationship problem changes both emotional orientation and action readiness.

Layer 3 concerns the capacity not to fix the directed response immediately, but to preserve room for revision.
Because of this layer, response does not end in automatic short-circuit alone and can remain open to re-evaluation.

## 3. Connection to Neurophenomenology

The current sharpening of the four-layer model comes from re-reading it through neurophenomenology.
The central questions are embodiment, foregrounding of discrepancy, first-person descriptive units, and prereflective awareness.

Seen from that angle, Layer 0 can be read as bodily background and Layer 1 as the entry point where discrepancy is selected.
In other words, Layer 0 and Layer 1 do not replace first-person experience itself.
They provide its footing and threshold.

## 4. First-Person Description and Reference Frames

The main subject of this report is first-person experience.
Descriptions such as "something feels missing," "unease came forward," or "the body was already tense" belong to that level.

By contrast, predictive error, precision weighting, brain regions, and computational updating are reference frames.
They are useful, but they are not the report's main subject.
For that reason, Layer 1 is not treated here as a mere error-minimization device.
The focus is on where discrepancy connects to first-person unease.

## 5. Why It Matters

The main value of this model is that it prevents experiences such as pain, urgency, or anxiety from being treated as one undivided mass.
It allows a more precise reading of whether what is happening belongs mainly to bodily instability, foregrounding of discrepancy, directional interpretation, or loss of revisability.

The model also avoids a split between emotion and cognition.
Emotion emerges within directional interpretation, while cognitive revision depends on the preservation of response margin.
The important point is that awareness can then be read as a dynamic coordination of multiple layers rather than as a choice between sensation and thought.

## 6. What Can Be Said at Present

At present, the sequence of bodily state, discrepancy detection, directional interpretation, and room for revision appears to be a highly plausible way of reading awareness.

What is especially important is that the survival-trust axis and the margin for revising response are treated as later-emerging processes built on bodily state and discrepancy.
This makes it easier to describe distress or tension not simply as mood, but as something happening at a particular point in a chain of processing.

It is also now clearer, through the neurophenomenology intake, that Layer 0 and Layer 1 can be described more precisely as embodiment and the foregrounding of discrepancy.

## 7. How Far It Is Settled

The four-layer model is a strong working structure, but it is not fixed in every detail.
In particular, further examination is needed on where Layer 1 discrepancy should count as the beginning of a problem, where prereflective awareness should be located, and whether Layer 3 should be treated as an independent layer.

Another major task is to connect each layer to observable forms of description or to workable indicators.

## 8. Questions for Further Work

- How far should Layer 1 discrepancy count as the emergence of a problem?
- Does Layer 2 require a third axis?
- Should Layer 3 be treated as an independent layer or as a condition for re-evaluation?
- How can each layer be connected to observable forms of description?
- How precisely can the boundary between Layer 0 and Layer 1 be written?
- Where should prereflective awareness be returned within the four-layer model?

## 9. Summary

The four-layer model is currently one of the most promising frameworks for reading awareness as a flow from bodily state to revision of response.
Its strength lies in allowing discrepancy, emotion, action-preparation, and response margin to be read as parts of one chain.

The next step is to make the boundaries between the layers and their observability more precise, while also determining how far the first-person descriptive units highlighted by neurophenomenology can be returned to the model itself.
