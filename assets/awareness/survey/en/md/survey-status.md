# Awareness Model Exploration: Survey Overview

## 1. Purpose of the Exploration

This exploration examines a model that places intersubjectivity — mutual understanding with others — at the center of awareness, building on existing findings from neurophenomenology, psychology, and philosophy.

The core hypothesis is:

> Signals related to intersubjectivity are processed by the nervous system with roughly the same weight as signals related to survival.

This is not a claim that intersubjectivity ranks "second only to money." It is a view that it matters at a level equivalent to life itself. This is not a rejection of capitalism or economic rationality; rather, it is an attempt to position survival and intersubjectivity as co-equal foundational dimensions.

## 2. Structure of the Exploration

The exploration is organized into three broad layers.

### Established Findings (General Foundation)

Findings established in prior research are organized here. No original claims are included.

- **Body and awareness**: Interoception provides the foundation of awareness (Craig 2002, Damasio 1994)
- **The predicting brain**: The free energy principle (Friston 2010)
- **Emotion**: The debate between constructionism (Barrett 2017) and basic emotion theory (Panksepp 1998), and where it currently stands
- **The social brain**: Social Baseline Theory (Coan 2015), social pain circuitry (Eisenberger 2003)

### Circumstantial Evidence for the Core Hypothesis

Circumstantial evidence spanning multiple levels of analysis — molecular, endocrine, neural circuit, psychological, epidemiological, evolutionary, and social/historical — is collected and evaluated here. No single piece of evidence proves the hypothesis independently, but together they point to the same intersection from different angles, like the blind scholars and the elephant.

Key sources: Cole (2007), Smith & Wang (2014), Eisenberger (2003), Panksepp (1998), Coan & Sbarra (2015), Baumeister & Leary (1995), Holt-Lunstad (2010, 2015), Dunbar (1998), Durkheim (1897)

### Philosophical and Clinical Foundations

Traditions that position intersubjectivity as constitutive rather than optional are referenced here as "perspectives pointing in the same direction" — not as evidence, but as conceptual convergence points.

Three thinkers closest to the core: Merleau-Ponty (intercorporeality), Stolorow (intersubjectivity and trauma), Nagarjuna / Heart Sutra (dependent origination / non-duality)
