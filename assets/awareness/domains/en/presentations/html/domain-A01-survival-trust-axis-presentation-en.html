Domain A01

Survival-Trust Axis

Intersubjective signals matter at the same level as survival

Source: what-is-consciousness.md · DRAFT-awareness-model-v3.md

Overview & Definition

Core Hypothesis

Among cognitive signals that rise from the nervous system into awareness, signals related to intersubjectivity (trust) are processed with approximately the same weight as survival-related signals.

Not "second most important after money," but important at the level of life itself — this is the antithesis of Domain A01.

Definition of the Two Axes

  • Survival Axis: Orientation toward avoiding danger, securing safety, defense, and protecting resources or standing
  • Trust Axis: Orientation toward reading whether relationships are maintained, whether connection can continue, whether repair is possible

These two axes are not a list of emotion names, but coordinates for discerning what a person is trying to protect and which relationships they are trying to maintain. The axes can be distinguished but, like yin and yang, cannot ultimately be separated.

Citation count

  • Coan & Sbarra (2015) Social Baseline Theory: Social resources are treated as "bioenergeticresources equivalent to oxygen or glucose." Holding a partner's hand reduces threat-related neural activity (Coan et al. 2006). Coan & Sbarra, Current Opinion in Psychology 2015

Shared Neural Circuitry: Physical and Social Pain

Eisenberger et al. (2003)

During social exclusion (Cyberball task), neural circuits partially shared with physical pain (dACC) were activated. fMRI study, N=13.

Eisenberger et al., Science, 302(5643), 290-292. (2003)

Debate and Current Consensus

  • Role of dACC: debate continues (general salience detection vs. shared pain)
  • Woo et al. (2014) MVPA: patterns are separable at pattern level (92% classification accuracy)
  • Current consensus: "different signals that partially share the same urgency channel" (v3 §2.2)

More important than the definitional debate of "pain vs. suffering" is the antithesis: physical survival is not the only thing required for life.

PANIC/GRIEF System and Separation Distress

Panksepp (1998)

Among 7 evolutionarily conserved primary emotional systems, the PANIC/GRIEF system responds to separation from attachment figures. Loss of social connection triggers biological distress responses.

Panksepp, Affective Neuroscience. Oxford University Press. (1998)

Neural Substrates

  • Regions: PAG (periaqueductal gray), ACC, BNST
  • Modulators: endogenous opioids, oxytocin, prolactin
  • Separation distress vocalizations are evolutionarily conserved indicators

The Debate with Barrett

van Heijst et al. (2025): Panksepp and Barrett are "irreconcilable but complementary." Even under Barrett's constructionist interpretation, the survival-level importance of intersubjective signals is not contested.

Summary

Established Findings

  • Social relationships directly relate to survival and health (Holt-Lunstad; Baumeister & Leary)
  • The brain defaults to assuming social proximity (Coan & Sbarra SBT)
  • Social exclusion and separation trigger biological distress responses (Eisenberger; Panksepp)

Position of the Core Hypothesis

These findings converge like blind scholars describing an elephant: from different angles, each points to the same intersection. They provide circumstantial support for the core hypothesis that intersubjective signals matter at the level of life itself. No research directly refuting this hypothesis has been found across 30 fields of investigation (v3 §2.3).
